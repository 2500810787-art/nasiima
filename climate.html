Understanding the Problem

What Drives Climate Change?

The primary driver is human activity — burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes release greenhouse gases that trap heat in our atmosphere.

01

Fossil Fuel Combustion

Coal, oil, and natural gas burning releases CO₂ and methane — accounting for over 75% of global greenhouse gas emissions. Power plants, vehicles, and industry are the biggest contributors.

02

Deforestation

Forests absorb CO₂. When trees are cleared for agriculture or logging, stored carbon is released and Earth loses a critical carbon sink. We lose about 15 billion trees each year.

03

Industrial Agriculture

Livestock produce methane through digestion. Rice paddies, synthetic fertilizers, and land clearing for farming collectively contribute 18–20% of global greenhouse gas emissions.

04

Industrial Processes

Cement production, steel manufacturing, and chemical processing release CO₂ and other potent greenhouse gases, many of which have warming potential thousands of times greater than CO₂.

05

Waste & Landfills

Decomposing organic waste in landfills produces methane. Poor waste management, open burning, and wastewater treatment add significant greenhouse gases to the atmosphere annually.

06

Transportation

Cars, trucks, ships, and aeroplanes burn petroleum fuels and are responsible for approximately 16% of global CO₂ emissions — with air travel being one of the fastest-growing sources.

Consequences

How It Impacts
Our World

Rising temperatures trigger a cascade of effects — from melting ice caps and extreme weather to food insecurity and mass displacement of communities.

+1.2°C
since pre-industrial era
🌊

Rising Sea Levels

Melting glaciers and thermal expansion of seawater are raising sea levels at 3.6mm per year. Coastal cities and island nations face flooding, erosion, and eventual submersion — threatening millions of lives.

🔥

Extreme Weather Events

Warmer oceans fuel stronger hurricanes, droughts, and heatwaves. The frequency of Category 4–5 tropical cyclones has increased significantly and record-breaking temperatures are set almost every year.

🧊

Melting Arctic Ice

Arctic sea ice is shrinking at 13% per decade. This disrupts polar ecosystems and accelerates warming by reducing Earth's reflective surface — a dangerous feedback loop.

🌿

Biodiversity Loss

Species unable to adapt fast enough are facing extinction. Coral reefs — which support 25% of marine life — are bleaching at unprecedented rates. Over 1 million species are now at risk.

🍽️

Food & Water Insecurity

Changing rainfall patterns, prolonged droughts, and crop failures are reducing agricultural yields. 2 billion people already face water scarcity — a number expected to double by 2050.

🏚️

Climate Displacement

Floods, droughts, and extreme heat force communities from their homes. The World Bank projects up to 216 million internal climate migrants by 2050 — disproportionately affecting the Global South.

Pathways Forward

Solutions That Work

The technology and knowledge to solve the climate crisis already exists. What's needed is unprecedented collective will — from governments, businesses, and individuals alike.

SOLUTION 01

Renewable Energy

Transitioning from fossil fuels to solar, wind, hydro, and geothermal power is the single biggest lever we have. Renewables now provide the cheapest electricity in history.

Energy
SOLUTION 02

Electrify Transport

Replacing combustion engines with electric vehicles — powered by clean grids — dramatically cuts transport emissions. Public transit expansion also reduces per-capita carbon footprints.

Transport
SOLUTION 03

Reforestation

Planting and protecting forests can sequester up to 10 billion tons of CO₂ per year. Protecting existing old-growth forests is even more critical — they store centuries of carbon.

Nature
SOLUTION 04

Sustainable Agriculture

Regenerative farming, reduced meat consumption, and precision agriculture reduce emissions while improving food security. Plant-rich diets could cut food-related emissions by 70%.

Food
SOLUTION 05

Green Buildings

Buildings consume 40% of global energy. Retrofitting insulation, switching to heat pumps, and designing net-zero structures can massively reduce emissions from heating, cooling, and lighting.

Infrastructure
SOLUTION 06

Climate Policy & Justice

Carbon pricing, phasing out fossil fuel subsidies, and international agreements like the Paris Accord create the systemic change needed. Climate justice ensures solutions don't burden the most vulnerable.

Policy
